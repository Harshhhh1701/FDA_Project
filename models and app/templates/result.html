WHAT IS A STROKE?

Here is all you need to know!

Stroke is a medical disorder in which the blood arteries in the brain are ruptured, causing damage to the brain. When the supply of blood and other nutrients to the brain is interrupted, symptoms might develop. According to the World Health Organization (WHO), stroke is the greatest cause of death and disability globally. Early recognition of the various warning signs of a stroke can help reduce the severity of the stroke.

WHY DOES A STROKE AFFECT DIFFERENT PARTS OF THE BODY?

Nerve cells in the brain tissue communicate with other cells to control functions including memory, speech and movement. When a stroke occurs, nerve cells in the brain tissue become injured. As a result of this injury, nerve cells cannot communicate with other cells, and functions are impaired. If a stroke occurs on the right side of the brain, the left side of the body is affected, and vice versa.

DIAGNOSING A STROKE

If your doctor suspects you're experiencing a stroke, they'll do tests to assist them figure out what's wrong. Certain tests can also be used to detect the type of stroke. First, your doctor will do a physical examination. They'll put your mental acuity, coordination, and balance to test. They'll be looking for:

  • Tingling or numbness in your face, arms, or legs
  • Signs of confusion
  • Speaking Difficulties
  • Sudden Vision Impairment

ACTING F.A.S.T IS IMPORTANT FOR STROKE

Acting F.A.S.T. can assist stroke patients in receiving the care they require. The most
effective stroke treatments are only possible if the stroke is noticed and diagnosed within three hours of the onset of symptoms.
Stroke patients may be ineligible if they do not arrive at the hospital on time.

If you suspect someone is having a stroke, react quickly and perform the following F.A.S.T test-

  • F—Face: Ask the person to smile. Does one side of the face droop?
  • A—Arms: Ask the person to raise both arms. Does one arm drift downward?
  • S—Speech: Ask the person to repeat a simple phrase. Is the speech slurred or strange?
  • T—Time: If you see any of these signs, call emergency number right away.

Note the time when any symptoms first appear. This information helps health care providers determine the best treatment for each person. Do not drive to the hospital or let someone else drive you. Call an ambulance so that medical personnel can begin life-saving treatment on the way to the emergency room.

SUPPLYING THE BRAIN WITH BLOOD

The brain is pumped with blood by two pairs of major arteries:

  • Internal carotid arteries that transport blood from the heart to the front of the neck.
  • The vertebral arteries that deliver blood from the heart to the back of the neck.

ABOUT

To the World Health Organization (WHO), stroke is the 2nd leading cause of death globally, responsible for approximately 11% of total deaths. This dataset is used to predict whether a patient is likely to get stroke based on the input parameters like gender, age, various diseases, and smoking status. Each row in the data provides relavant information about the patient.

Using the Stroke Prediction dataset from Kaggle, our team was able to use machine learning to get a 94.77% accuracy in predicting the chances of a person getting a stroke. The factors taken into consideration from the datasets were age, gender: biological male & female, having pre-existing hypertension, pre-existing heart disease, marriage status, work type, residence: urban or rural, glucose level, BMI, and smoking status.
We used models like Logistic Regression, Decision Tree, Random Forest & AdaBoost, K neighbors, and support vector machine to test the maximum accuracy we can achieve.
